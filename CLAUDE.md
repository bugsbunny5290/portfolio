# Portfolio - Pranav Gautam

## Design Context

### Users
- **Primary audience**: Hiring managers, recruiters, and engineering leaders evaluating Pranav as a Staff Engineer candidate
- **Secondary audience**: Professional peers, collaborators, and tech community contacts
- **Context of use**: Typically visiting from a job application, LinkedIn profile, or referral link — scanning quickly, often on mobile, forming a first impression in seconds
- **Job to be done**: Quickly assess technical depth, seniority, and cultural fit; download CV in preferred format (PDF/Word, EN/DE)

### Brand Personality
- **Voice**: Bold, Modern, Dynamic
- **Tone**: Confident and direct — speaks through quality of craft rather than self-promotion
- **Emotional goals**: Visitors should feel trust and credibility ("this is a serious, senior engineer"), calm and clarity ("everything is well-organized"), and be impressed and inspired ("this person cares about craft and quality")

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: High-energy tech aesthetic with strong contrasts and forward-looking feel, grounded by clean structure
- **Reference**: GitHub profile style — familiar developer aesthetic with clean cards, tech-forward presentation, contribution-graph energy
- **Anti-references**: Overly corporate/templated portfolios; cluttered designs with competing visual elements; generic Bootstrap-style layouts
- **Theme**: Light and dark modes with system detection; HSL-based color system with blue primary
- **Typography**: Geist + Geist Mono — technical precision with modern warmth
- **Color palette**: Blue primary (#5B6FB3 light / #9DC4FF dark), off-white/dark navy backgrounds, clean card surfaces

### Design Principles
1. **Craft signals seniority** — Every pixel, transition, and layout decision should demonstrate engineering rigor. The portfolio itself is the first code review.
2. **Clarity over decoration** — Bold doesn't mean noisy. Strong visual hierarchy, generous whitespace, and intentional contrast guide the eye without clutter.
3. **Accessible by default** — Target WCAG AAA compliance: enhanced contrast ratios (7:1 for text), respect `prefers-reduced-motion`, comprehensive screen reader support, visible focus indicators on all interactive elements.
4. **Mobile-first, always** — Every component must work flawlessly on mobile before being enhanced for larger screens. No layout should break or feel cramped on small viewports.
5. **Bilingual parity** — EN and DE experiences should be equally polished. German text (often longer) must be accounted for in all layout decisions.
